# MAIR Thesis – Structured Feedback and Recommended Next Steps

## 1. Overall Direction and Core Focus

This project examines **state hedging behavior** under conditions of intensified Sino–US strategic competition (circa 2016–present), with a regional focus on **South and Southeast Asia**.  
The core empirical interest lies in how *middle powers* respond to heightened structural pressure when alignment choices are costly and uncertain.

A promising design centers on a **small-N comparative study** of ASEAN member states, with **Vietnam** as the primary case and **the Philippines** and **Indonesia** as secondary comparative cases. ASEAN membership helps hold constant supranational institutional context while allowing for meaningful variation in strategic behavior.

---

## 2. Clarifying the Research Question

The research question should be specified more precisely. A working formulation could be:

> *How do middle powers in South and Southeast Asia hedge between the United States and China during the period of intensified Sino–US competition since 2016?*

This question can be refined further once the theoretical expectations are clarified. Importantly, the question should foreground **observable behavior**, not abstract preference.

---

## 3. Theoretical Motivation and Puzzle

Rather than relying on a simple “gap in the literature” argument, the motivation should emerge from **unresolved theoretical tensions** in the hedging literature.

The literature review should:
- Identify **competing expectations** about hedging behavior under contemporary geopolitical conditions.
- Show that existing theories offer **divergent or incomplete predictions** about how middle powers behave when great-power rivalry intensifies.
- Use these tensions to define a **theoretical puzzle** that justifies the study.

This theoretical puzzle should then be summarized concisely in the introduction as the study’s core motivation.

---

## 4. Literature Review Structure

The literature review should be analytically structured, not descriptive.

### 4.1 Hedging in International Relations
- Define hedging and clarify how it differs from:
  - Balancing
  - Bandwagoning
- Engage explicitly with the debate over whether hedging is:
  - A distinct strategic posture, or
  - A composite of balancing and bandwagoning behaviors.

### 4.2 Neorealist and Defensive Realist Foundations
- Start from core structural arguments about uncertainty and power asymmetry.
- Trace how expectations about middle-power behavior under uncertainty develop from early structural realism through later defensive realist work.
- Emphasize how these approaches generate **baseline expectations** for hedging.

### 4.3 Alternative and Modified Approaches
- Introduce at least one additional framework, such as:
  - Modified realist approaches
  - Institutional or constructivist perspectives
- Clarify how these alternatives lead to **different expectations** about when, how, and why states hedge.

### 4.4 Conceptual Clarifications
Address two key conceptual issues explicitly:
- **Middle power**:  
  - Materialist definitions versus broader role-based or institutional definitions.
  - Justify which definition is adopted and why it fits the cases selected.
- **Hedging as the core variable**:  
  - Specify what counts as evidence of hedging behavior.

---

## 5. Case Selection and Research Design

### 5.1 Case Selection Logic
The methodology chapter should clearly justify:
- Why Vietnam is the primary case.
- Why the Philippines and Indonesia are appropriate comparative cases.
- How ASEAN membership functions as a partial control on institutional context.

A table summarizing key structural and political characteristics of each case is strongly recommended.

### 5.2 Data and Sources
Specify:
- What types of data will be used (e.g. policy documents, defense cooperation agreements, diplomatic actions, economic agreements).
- The time frame for data collection.
- Criteria for selecting relevant events.

---

## 6. Empirical Structure

The empirical core of the thesis will likely consist of **three country-focused sections or chapters**.

For each case:
- Identify a limited number of **salient events** relevant to Sino–US competition.
- Events should be comparable in strategic significance, even if not identical.
- Analyze state behavior in each event using the hedging framework developed earlier.

The goal is not exhaustive coverage, but **analytical depth and comparability**.

---

## 7. Discussion and Conclusion

The concluding section should:
- Summarize empirical findings.
- Evaluate them against the theoretical expectations developed in the literature review.
- Clarify the study’s contribution to:
  - The hedging literature
  - Debates on middle powers in contemporary geopolitics
- Discuss limitations and avenues for future research.

---

## 8. Key Next Steps

1. Sharpen the definition of hedging and middle power.
2. Reframe the literature review around a clear theoretical puzzle.
3. Refine the research question in light of competing expectations.
4. Specify observable indicators of hedging behavior.
5. Finalize case selection and event criteria.

This sequence should guide the next phase of thesis development.
