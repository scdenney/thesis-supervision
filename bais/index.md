# BAIS — International Studies

<span class="card-level ba">BA</span>

The BA thesis in International Studies is a 10,000-word research paper representing the capstone of your undergraduate studies. The thesis should demonstrate your ability to formulate a research question with a global perspective, engage with relevant scholarship, and apply appropriate methods to produce original academic work.

---

## Key Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Word count** | 10,000 words (±10%) |
| **Citation style** | Chicago Notes-Bibliography |
| **Language** | English |
| **Special requirement** | Global/comparative perspective expected |
| **Final deadline** | June 23, 2026 |

---

## Key Deadlines (2025–2026, Semester 2)

| Milestone | Deadline |
|-----------|----------|
| Research question due | Week 9 |
| Literature review due | Week 12 |
| Draft submission | Week 18 |
| **Final thesis** | **June 23, 2026** |

Always confirm exact dates with your supervisor and check Brightspace for updates.

---

## Assessment

Your thesis is assessed by a first reader (supervisor) and a second reader using the faculty's shared assessment criteria:

1. **Knowledge and Insight** — Research question, motivation, and literature review
2. **Application of Knowledge** — Data, methods, and analysis
3. **Reaching Conclusions** — Synthesis, contribution, and limitations
4. **Communication** — Writing, structure, and citations

See the [Shared Assessment Standards]({{ '/shared-standards/' | relative_url }}) page for detailed criteria and grade descriptors.

---

## What's Expected at BA Level

A strong BA thesis demonstrates that you can:

- Identify a clear research question and a gap in the existing literature
- Engage meaningfully with relevant scholarship
- Apply a suitable methodology and present your findings clearly
- Write in competent academic English with proper citations

You are not expected to make a groundbreaking original contribution, but you should show the capacity for independent research. See [BA vs MA Expectations]({{ '/shared-standards/#ba-vs-ma-expectations' | relative_url }}) for a detailed comparison.
